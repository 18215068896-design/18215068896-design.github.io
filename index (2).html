Sodium Caseinate

A Safe Food Additive and Nutritional Enhancer

What is Sodium Caseinate?

Sodium caseinate (also known as casein sodium) is the sodium salt of casein, the main protein in cow's milk. It is produced by treating casein curd with an alkaline substance (such as sodium hydroxide), converting insoluble casein into a soluble form, resulting in a white or light yellow powder or granules.

It is rich in essential amino acids, with protein content typically over 90%, offering high nutritional value. It also provides excellent emulsifying, thickening, and stabilizing properties, making it widely used as a food additive.

Appearance: White to light yellow powder or granules

Main Properties

  • Soluble in water; aqueous solution is neutral or slightly alkaline; precipitates with acid.
  • High viscosity, excellent emulsification, thickening, foaming, and heat stability (resistant to high-temperature sterilization).
  • Contains both hydrophilic and hydrophobic groups, effectively stabilizing emulsions.
  • Odorless or with a slight characteristic smell; strong heat stability.

Production Process

Using fresh milk or skim milk as raw material:

  1. Precipitate casein curd using acid or enzyme.
  2. Add sodium hydroxide or sodium carbonate to neutralize, converting casein to soluble sodium salt.
  3. Evaporate and spray-dry or freeze-dry to produce powder.

Complies with food safety standards (e.g., GB 1886.212-2016 in China; similar international regulations).

Main Applications

Widely used in the food industry as an emulsifier, thickener, stabilizer, and nutritional enhancer:

  • Meat products (e.g., ham, sausages): Improves water retention, emulsifies fat, enhances texture.
  • Dairy products (e.g., ice cream, yogurt, coffee creamer): Stabilizes emulsions, prevents separation.
  • Baked goods (e.g., bread, cookies): Adds nutrition, improves mouthfeel.
  • Beverages (e.g., protein drinks): Increases protein content.
  • Other: Cosmetics and pharmaceuticals as stabilizers.

Safety

Sodium caseinate is recognized by FAO/WHO as a safe food additive with no specific ADI (Acceptable Daily Intake) limit required. It is permitted worldwide.

Note: Derived from milk; avoid if allergic to milk proteins. Generally tolerable for lactose-intolerant individuals (most lactose removed during processing).
